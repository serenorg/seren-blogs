Most sales tools are built to measure pressure. BAT Sales Coach is built to protect momentum. The skill turns sales coaching into a repeatable operating loop that starts with what actually happened in the pipeline, reinforces real completed work, and only then moves into technique planning. BAT stands for `Behavior`, `Attitude`, and `Technique`, and that ordering is the point.

That simple design choice changes the entire tone of a coaching session. The [BAT Sales Coach skill](https://github.com/serenorg/seren-skills/tree/main/sales/bat-sales-coach) acts like a lightweight personal CRM, reflective journal, and supportive sales coach inside SerenDesktop. It helps a sales executive log prospect work, reconnect to what is in motion, and choose the next step without turning the conversation into punishment disguised as productivity. If you are building pipeline in a volatile market, that matters more than another dashboard.

It also stays grounded in reality. It records prospect names, stages, task-style behavior entries, responses, next actions, and timing that the user actually states. Follow-up dates are treated as user-stated facts, not agent-generated guesses, which keeps the session focused on selling instead of untangling made-up scheduling.

## Top 3 Features of BAT Sales Coach

### 1. Behavior-first sales coaching

BAT Sales Coach begins with action, not abstraction. It captures concrete pipeline work like outreach, follow-ups, proposals, scheduling, event sourcing, and account movement. That makes the session feel closer to a living CRM than a vague pep talk.

### 2. An attitude loop that lowers noise

After behavior is recorded, the skill moves into attitude. It asks for a score from `1` to `10`, asks where that feeling lives in the body, and then asks a blunt question: `Can you tell the future?` The goal is to break the habit of treating uncertainty like proof of failure.

### 3. Self-directed technique planning

Technique comes last, and only after behavior is complete and curiosity is present. The skill helps the user choose a technique experiment for the next cycle, select practice areas, and define the next behavior target for active prospects.

## How the BAT loop works in practice

The best way to think about the skill is as a sequence that protects signal from emotion without pretending emotion is irrelevant.

First comes `Behavior`. The coach asks what was planned, what was actually done, whether anything else counts as a win, what the prospect said or did, and what comes next. This makes progress visible and gives the work structure.

Second comes `Attitude`. This is where the skill helps the user notice how they feel without letting feeling rewrite reality. A tough prospect response, a delayed close, or a quiet week does not automatically mean the pipeline is dead. By insisting that the future cannot be known, the skill helps the user return to curiosity instead of catastrophizing.

Third comes `Technique`. Only after the coaching loop has a factual base does the skill help the user think about experiments: change the outreach angle, tighten discovery questions, improve follow-up framing, or revisit a proposal.

This order is what makes the skill feel different from generic sales automation. It does not flatten the user into a quota. It treats selling as a process of observed behavior, emotional regulation, and deliberate experimentation.
